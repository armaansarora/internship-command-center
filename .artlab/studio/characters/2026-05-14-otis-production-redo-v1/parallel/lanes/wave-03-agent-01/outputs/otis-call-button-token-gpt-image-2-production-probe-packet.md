## Strongest Specificity

Give Otis one tiny, thumb-polished brass elevator-call button token on a short vest watch chain. It is not magic, not a joke prop, and not a large medallion. It is a believable concierge habit: he rubs the worn call-button token when thinking, checks it before greeting, and lets it catch one small warm highlight in the Lobby lighting.

The token should read as "this man has opened this building for thousands of people" at desktop scale, but disappear into the costume as a premium brass detail at mobile scale.

## Why This Is Usable

- It reinforces Otis as threshold keeper instead of adding random whimsy.
- It stays inside the approved brass/keycard/ledger prop family.
- It supports CharacterStage motion states through a small, repeatable hand gesture.
- It gives the coordinator one memorable identity hook without changing face, body, outfit, or silhouette.
- It can be rejected cleanly if it becomes too weird, oversized, glowing, or fantasy-coded.
