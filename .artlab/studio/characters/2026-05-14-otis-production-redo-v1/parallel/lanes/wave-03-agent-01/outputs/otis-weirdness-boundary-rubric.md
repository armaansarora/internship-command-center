# Otis Weirdness Boundary Rubric

Lane: wave-03-agent-01
Specificity: tiny brass elevator-call button token on vest watch chain

## Green Zone - Ship-Ready Weird

Approve the idea for coordinator probe generation if all are true:

- The token is smaller than Otis's palm and smaller than any keycard or ledger prop.
- It reads as old matte brass, thumb-polished by use, not shiny jewelry.
- It is attached to a short vest watch chain or mostly hidden under the formal coat.
- It supports one adult habit: a tiny thumb rub, chain check, or brief pause before greeting.
- Otis's face, soft Santa warmth, slight belly, posture, and silhouette remain the main read.
- At mobile size, the token becomes a warm brass speck or chain cue, not a separate focal object.

## Yellow Zone - Rework Before Expansion

Regenerate or revise prompt wording if any are true:

- The token is readable only because it is too large.
- It competes with the hand gesture or breaks the silhouette.
- The chain tangles with fingers, beard, vest buttons, or ledger props.
- It starts to look like jewelry instead of an old concierge object.
- The generator adds extra tokens, extra chains, logos, numbers, readable text, or unexplained symbols.
- The formal outfit becomes cluttered because the token is forced too visible.

## Red Zone - Hard Reject

Reject the direction immediately if any are true:

- The token glows, emits light, or looks magical.
- It becomes an amulet, pendant, talisman, religious icon, or fantasy keymaster object.
- It becomes a steampunk device, sci-fi gadget, neon button, or tech wearable.
- It is larger than Otis's palm or becomes the main silhouette feature.
- Otis's behavior becomes goofy, mascot-like, mystical, childish, or prop-obsessed.
- The object forces cropped hands, malformed fingers, noisy chains, or production-risk overlaps.

## One-Sentence Rejection Rule

If the call-button token stops looking like a tiny real-world concierge habit and starts looking like a magic object, costume gimmick, or silhouette-dominating gag, reject the probe and remove the specificity from production.

## Coordinator Note

This idea should be treated as a small identity hook, not a redesign. If it fails restraint, the safest merge path is to keep only the gesture idea: "Otis briefly checks his vest chain before greeting" with no visible token.
