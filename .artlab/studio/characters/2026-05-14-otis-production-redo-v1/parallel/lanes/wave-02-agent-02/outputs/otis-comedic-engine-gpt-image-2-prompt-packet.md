## Strongest Direction

Make Otis funny through tiny, competent human timing: he is the threshold keeper who has already anticipated the user's next need, occasionally one beat too early, and then self-corrects with warmth. The comedy lives in a half-smile, a paused hand, a lifted brow, a slightly overprepared ledger, and the quiet dignity of a man who has seen every lobby problem twice.

This is not joke design. Otis must remain adult, premium, trustworthy, slightly soft-bellied, lived-in, and concierge-competent. The funny recurring behavior is "warm overpreparedness," not silliness.

## Source Contract For Every Probe

- Generate one sprite or one review sheet per source image. Do not use multi-pose sheets as production sprite sources.
- Native source must have long edge at least 4096 px before derivative processing.
- Prefer transparent RGBA output. If unavailable, use a clean mask workflow; do not rely on chroma-key unless preflight proves a truly flat background.
- Full body sprites need 10 to 14 percent safe padding with both hands, both feet, beard edge, jacket hem, and any small prop fully visible.
- Attach the approved Otis identity reference as the highest-priority reference if available.
- Preserve the approved soft Santa concierge identity without holiday costume, childish mascot energy, or fake-perfect AI model polish.
- Keep all generated files inside the parent run until coordinator review and final approval. No public/art writes.

## Comedy Restraint Rules

1. Expression delta stays small: 10 to 20 percent more expressive than canonical calm Otis.
2. Otis is never the butt of the joke. The humor comes from his gentle preparation and timing.
3. No wink, tongue, open-mouth laugh, cartoon eyebrow arch, shrugging punchline, exaggerated confusion, big grin, or toy-like proportions.
4. The user must read "I am safe with him" before "he is amusing."
5. Every funny beat must still work as a useful app state at 96 to 160 px tall.
