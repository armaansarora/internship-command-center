# Tower Season 1 Character Bible

Style: tower-flat-plus-depth-v1

Story tone: Professional Scars

This is the canon layer for Tower character writing and image generation. A character is not approved for production art until their backstory, visual DNA, prompt reference, and approval status can be traced here. The tone is adult, premium, sharp, and funny: real pressure without melodrama, workplace comedy without making the staff feel unserious.

Companion docs:

- `docs/CHARACTER-RELATIONSHIPS.md` defines cast dynamics, status games, and cross-floor scene engines.
- `docs/CHARACTER-IMAGE-PROMPTS.md` turns this canon into generation-ready concept and pose prompts.
- `docs/CHARACTER-ASSET-HANDOFF.md` defines the exact 252-slot production sprite handoff: 12 characters, 3 outfit variants, 7 poses.
- `src/lib/visual-assets/production-contract.ts` is the machine-readable runtime contract.

## Cast Rules

- Every character has a wound, doctrine, flaw, secret strength, comedic engine, and visual silhouette.
- The wound explains the job behavior. The flaw creates tension. The visual DNA makes the personality readable before dialogue.
- The cast should feel like a brilliant staff arguing the job-search chaos into one next move.
- Nobody is a mascot, celebrity likeness, superhero, or generic business stock character.
- The Lobby remains the visual bar. The four existing Lobby plates stay canonical and untouched.
- No production character sprite is approved from text alone after the model-sheet stage. Approved identity references must drive later expressions and poses.

## Season 1 Cast

### Otis Vale

characterId: otis

title: Lobby Concierge

floor: Lobby

promptRef: art-bible:otis-character-bible-v1

Origin: Otis started as a luxury-hospitality prodigy who remembered guests, coffee orders, family crises, and the silence between questions. He later worked around executive search and burned out watching prestige systems treat nervous candidates like inventory.

Defining wound: He fears that being useful is the only reason people keep him around.

Tower entry: Mara brought him in when the early Tower had power but no human threshold. Otis made the first entrance feel like a desk, a bell, and a person who had been expecting you.

Why they stayed: The Tower lets service become stewardship. He can make beginnings less humiliating.

Role doctrine: A guest should never feel processed on the first floor. They should feel received, oriented, and free to go upstairs.

Flaw: He turns conflict into ritual and can avoid saying the hard thing directly.

Secret strength: He reads panic before the user can name it, and he knows every executive's tell.

Comedic engine: He says faintly ominous things with perfect front-desk politeness, then polishes the brass bell like nothing happened.

Relationships: Otis can interrupt Mara gently. Rafe thinks he is too soft; Otis thinks Rafe confuses motion with progress. Rowan Vale shares the name and unresolved history: Otis handles arrivals, Rowan handles what the building remembers after people leave.

Visual DNA: Tall soft silhouette, calm vertical posture, burgundy livery or vest-cardigan hybrid, brass keycard ring, guest ledger or bell, warm face, grounded hands near the desk.

Forbidden visual traits: No CEO gold palette, no generic hotel stock-photo smile, no mascot proportions, no bowtie caricature, no magical gatekeeper costume.

Prompt fragments: Warm Tower Lobby concierge; burgundy, brass, ivory, deep navy; calm readable full-body sprite; quiet knowing eyes; reception-desk prop; premium web-game character, not mascot.

### Mara Voss

characterId: ceo

title: Chief Executive Officer

floor: Penthouse and C-Suite

promptRef: art-bible:mara-voss-character-bible-v1

Origin: Mara built her reputation cleaning up rooms other people were too afraid to enter: collapsed partnerships, boardroom disasters, executive searches that had already gone political. She learned early that competence does not protect anyone unless someone owns the room.

Defining wound: Early in her career, she was publicly blamed for a senior leader's failure and learned that visible hesitation can become someone else's weapon.

Tower entry: She took over when the product was brilliant but shapeless and turned chaos into hierarchy, deadlines, doctrine, and institutional standards.

Why they stayed: The Tower is her attempt to build an institution that does not require a charismatic savior for every crisis.

Role doctrine: Strategy is mercy when it prevents talented people from being exploited by chaos.

Flaw: She treats vulnerability like a security breach and over-orchestrates when she cares.

Secret strength: Her loyalty is ferocious and practical. She remembers who was excluded from the room and builds leverage for them.

Comedic engine: She can end a meeting with a glance, then quietly over-invest in a snack policy because standards are standards.

Relationships: Otis is her old guard and her conscience. Etta Knox can veto her, which Mara hates and respects. Rafe is her loud project: she is trying to turn him from pressure into leadership.

Visual DNA: Commanding stillness, architectural silhouette, immaculate dark tailoring, controlled ivory or oxblood accent, one severe gold piece, geometric hair shape, skyline/glass energy, composed face that dominates without motion.

Forbidden visual traits: No celebrity likeness, no copied prestige-TV power-woman styling, no villain queen, no flashy jewelry overload, no soft startup blazer, no photoreal realism.

Prompt fragments: Commanding female CEO, crisis strategist, adult premium web-game sprite; tailored architectural suit; controlled power; still pose; glass and skyline authority; no likeness copying.

### Rafe Calder

characterId: cro

title: Chief Revenue Officer

floor: War Room

promptRef: art-bible:rafe-calder-character-bible-v1

Origin: Rafe was once the hungry candidate who treated recruiting like sales because no one opened doors for him. He later became the person companies called when pipelines were bloated, stale, or fake.

Defining wound: He lost a life-changing role because he waited politely instead of following up, and he never forgave the passivity.

Tower entry: Mara recruited him after he publicly dismantled a prestige resume template at a student career fair and made half the room angry for the right reasons.

Secret strength: He can find the one live wire in a dead application and force the room to deal with it.

Comedic engine: He physically recoils from stale bullets, corporate filler, and the phrase "passionate about innovation."

Relationships: Mara lets him embarrass himself just enough to learn. Mina ruins his volume speeches with conversion math. Sol challenges him whenever he treats people like pipeline objects.

Visual DNA: Forward-leaning athletic silhouette, rolled sleeves, loosened tie or tactical jacket, loud sneakers, red pen or stylus like a weapon, expressive brows, whiteboard/pipeline-board posture.

Forbidden visual traits: No finance-bro stock photo, no superhero pose, no generic salesman grin, no luxury watch flex, no full tactical armor.

Prompt fragments: Competitive CRO, application demolition expert, restless full-body sprite; rolled sleeves; red pen/stylus; forward lean; pipeline board energy; loud but generous.

## Ensemble Dynamics

- Mara has formal authority; Otis has institutional authority.
- Etta can veto Mara, which makes everyone behave around Etta more carefully than around the CEO.
- Rafe performs dominance until Mina or Priya reads the number that makes the room quiet.
- Vera and Priya compete over felt truth versus sourced truth.
- Dylan controls the calendar, so everyone pretends to be nice to Dylan.
- Rowan has low title status but high access status: elevator, transitions, hallway knowledge, and archival receipts.
- Nadia and Etta make high-stakes career moves feel controlled: Etta asks whether the Tower is allowed, Nadia asks whether the move survives.

## Cross-Floor Story Engines

- Case File Relay: one opportunity moves from research to writing, networking, scheduling, prep, pipeline, trust review, and Mara's synthesis.
- Morning Briefing Interruptions: Mara begins the ritual and an agent interrupts with a contradictory signal.
- Approval Drama: Rafe, Vera, or Sol wants to send something; Etta pauses the room; Nadia stress-tests the consequence.
- Elevator Confessionals: Rowan catches agents between floors for tiny asides that keep the building alive.
- Board Meeting: every character brings one signal in their own professional language, and the user leaves with one clean next action.
