## 🗂️ The bank, by role

### THE SYSTEM ITSELF
_the whole machine that turns any idea into a finished thing and improves itself — the name that replaces "factory"_

- **The Refinery** _(From 'refine' (Latin 're-' + 'fine'). The industrial plant that takes crude raw feedstock and processes it through staged units into a finished product.)_ — Wins on both axes the role demands. 'Crude in, finished product out, in continuous stages' nails idea-to-finished-thing, and 'refine' is the one word that literally names the self-improving polish loop — no other candidate encodes BOTH the production pipeline AND the improvement step in its plain meaning. A smart person hears 'Refinery' and immediately pictures a multi-stage machine that upgrades raw material. Highest combined descriptiveness without going antiquated.  `desc 9 · cool 6`
- **The Forge** _(Latin 'fabrica' (workshop) to Old French 'forge'. The hearth where raw metal is heated, shaped, and reworked until it becomes a finished tool.)_ — The self-improvement loop is baked into the metaphor: the smith re-heats and re-hammers the same piece until it's right, which is exactly the iterate-to-finished behavior. Raw input in, finished tool out — instantly legible. Edges out other craft names because 'reworking' (improvement) is intrinsic to forging, not just making.  `desc 8 · cool 8`
- **The Fabricator** _(Latin 'fabricare' (to construct, to frame). Modern engineering 'fab' = the plant that builds finished components, e.g. a semiconductor chip fab.)_ — Reads almost as literally as 'factory' — 'the thing that builds the thing' — while 'fab' carries a clean high-tech, precision-production connotation. Maximally guessable for the maker role; loses only because the self-improvement dimension isn't in the word itself.  `desc 9 · cool 6`
- **The Shipyard** _(Old English 'scip' + 'geard'. The yard where a vessel is laid down keel-to-launch and sent out into the world.)_ — Uniquely captures the full arc the role cares about: from bare keel (raw idea) to a launched, seaworthy ship that goes OUT into the world — the 'finished thing that ships' is right there, resonating with 'ship it.' More end-to-end than a workshop and more legible than the myth names.  `desc 8 · cool 7`
- **The Atelier** _(French, from Old French 'astelier' (a pile of wood splinters to a craftsman's workshop). A master's studio where work is conceived and finished under one roof.)_ — Telegraphs end-to-end making — conceived AND finished in one place — and the master-studio sense hints at refinement of the craft over time, which gestures at the improvement loop. Best balance of legible-maker meaning and genuine class among the studio names.  `desc 7 · cool 8`
- **The Mint** _(Latin 'moneta' (the temple where Roman coins were struck) to Old English 'mynet'. The facility that stamps raw bullion into finished, circulating currency.)_ — Turns formless raw value into a finished, standardized, ship-ready artifact at scale — strongly implies a repeatable production engine with a recognizable, stamped output. Short, sharp, and the 'standardized output at scale' reading is easy to guess.  `desc 7 · cool 8`
- **The Workshop** _(Old English 'weorc' + 'sceoppa' (shed/booth). The space where a craftsperson builds and finishes things by hand.)_ — The single most legible name in the field — virtually impossible to misread as anything other than 'a place where ideas get built into finished things.' Kept as the safe, maximally-descriptive anchor; its only weakness is low spark, and it carries no hint of the self-improvement loop.  `desc 10 · cool 4`

### THE RECURSIVE UNIT
_the self-similar cell that does one job and can spawn smaller copies of ITSELF for sub-problems — the onion layer, the thing-inside-a-thing_

- **Fractal** _(Mathematics (Mandelbrot, Latin 'fractus' = broken) — a structure where every part is a smaller copy of the whole, self-similar at any zoom level.)_ — The single most precise word for self-similarity: a thing whose sub-parts ARE smaller copies of itself, recursing at every zoom. High descriptiveness AND high cool — it telegraphs the nesting-of-self instantly and reads beautifully on a system diagram.  `desc 9 · cool 8`
- **Self-Spawner** _(Computing/gaming ('spawn' = instantiate a new instance) prefixed with 'self', describing a process that creates new instances of its own kind.)_ — Maximum descriptiveness — it states the core behavior outright (a unit that spawns more of itself for sub-problems) with zero ambiguity. The user's hard rule rewards exactly this kind of role-telegraphing.  `desc 10 · cool 5`
- **Recursor** _(Computer science (Latin 'recurrere' = to run back), the agent-noun form of recursion — a function that calls itself.)_ — Recursion IS the definition of this role. To anyone with a technical ear it names 'the thing that calls itself on sub-problems' — and the coined agent-noun gives it more edge than plain 'recursion'.  `desc 9 · cool 6`
- **Matryoshka** _(Russian nested dolls — each doll opens to reveal a smaller, identical doll inside.)_ — The most universal physical image of a thing-inside-an-identical-thing — the 'onion layer' made famous. Anyone hearing it pictures self-copies nested down, and it's strikingly cool. Crosses cultures, not just CS.  `desc 8 · cool 8`
- **Cell** _(Biology (Latin 'cella' = small chamber) — the smallest self-contained unit of life that divides into copies of itself (mitosis).)_ — One syllable that carries the whole role: the smallest self-contained unit that does one job and divides into copies of itself. Clean, real-term, and reinforces the 'spawn smaller me' behavior from a different (biological) source.  `desc 8 · cool 6`
- **Subdivider** _(Geometry/recursion ('subdivide' = split a shape into smaller copies of the same shape, as in recursive subdivision algorithms).)_ — Plainly names the mechanism: break a problem into smaller versions of the same shape and recurse on each. Strong descriptiveness and adds a geometry source distinct from the CS and biology picks.  `desc 9 · cool 5`
- **Homunculus** _(Alchemy/philosophy (Latin 'little human') — a miniature complete being; the homunculus argument is the classic 'a smaller copy inside doing the same job' regress.)_ — The 'little version of you inside doing your job' is exactly this role's image, and it's the highest-cool pick that still telegraphs function. Brings a myth/alchemy source for variety; the classic infinite-regress reading nails the recursive-self idea.  `desc 7 · cool 9`

### THE COMMANDER / ORCHESTRATOR
_the central brain you talk to; owns the run, spawns the workers, watches everything — the one in charge_

- **The Conductor** _(Latin 'conducere' (to lead together); the figure who stands before an orchestra, cues each section, and holds the whole performance in time.)_ — Near-perfect map to the role: reads instantly as 'the one coordinating many players in sync,' owns tempo and the whole without touching an instrument — exactly spawn + synchronize workers. High descriptiveness AND high cool, which is why it tops the generic 'Orchestrator.'  `desc 9 · cool 8`
- **The Showrunner** _(TV-industry term for the single person with creative + operational authority over an entire series — hires the room, owns the arc, ships every episode.)_ — Telegraphs 'one mind ultimately in charge of the whole production end to end,' explicitly including delegating to a staff and SHIPPING — which matches 'turns an idea into a finished thing' better than any other candidate. Descriptive and genuinely cool.  `desc 8 · cool 8`
- **The Dispatcher** _(Logistics/emergency services; the central node that receives the request, assigns units, and tracks every job to completion.)_ — Almost a one-to-one description of the function: takes the request, spawns/assigns the workers, watches them all to completion. Maximally on-function; only reads a touch reactive rather than 'in charge,' so it sits below Conductor/Showrunner.  `desc 9 · cool 6`
- **The Orchestrator** _(From 'orchestra'; in computing it's the literal term for the system that schedules and coordinates distributed work (Kubernetes, Airflow).)_ — The technically exact word — engineers hear it and know precisely what it does, and it's literally this role's name. Kept high for unbeatable descriptiveness; ranked here only because it's the generic term, not a found/characterful name.  `desc 10 · cool 5`
- **The Marshal** _(Frankish 'marah-skalk' (horse-servant) → highest military rank; the officer who marshals (assembles and orders) forces and directs the field.)_ — 'To marshal' literally means to assemble and direct a body of units, so the verb hidden in the title telegraphs orchestration, while the rank signals top authority. Both halves — coordinate AND command — land, with strong cool.  `desc 8 · cool 8`
- **The Overseer** _(Old English 'oferseon' (to look over); the one set above the work to watch and direct laborers — and the controlling AI in the Fallout vaults.)_ — Carries both halves explicitly: 'watches everything' and 'is in charge of the workers.' Function is unmistakable from the word itself; reads slightly authoritarian, which keeps it just under Marshal.  `desc 8 · cool 7`
- **The Foreman** _(Craft/construction; the worker who runs the crew on site — assigns tasks, sets pace, and answers for the build.)_ — Plainly 'the boss who directs the workers and owns the job,' strong on delegation and ownership of shipping a build. Edges out Quarterback/Helmsman because its descriptiveness needs no domain knowledge; loses to the top tier on cool.  `desc 8 · cool 6`

### THE INTAKE / DECIDER
_decides WHAT to build and whether it is worth building; turns a fuzzy wish into a sharp job_

- **The Scope Arbiter** _('Scope' from project management (defining the bounds of a job); 'arbiter' from Latin for one who settles/decides.)_ — Best two-word capture of BOTH halves of the role: 'Scope' = bound the fuzzy wish into a defined job, 'Arbiter' = render the go/no-go verdict. Nothing wasted, nothing vague, and it reads sharp.  `desc 9 · cool 7`
- **The Intake Magistrate** _(Latin magistratus, 'public official / one who judges'; 'intake' from triage/admissions language (hospitals, asylum cases).)_ — 'Intake' nails the receive-the-raw-wish entry point; 'Magistrate' nails sitting in judgment on it. Together they telegraph 'take in a request, rule on whether it proceeds' — and the pairing is genuinely cool.  `desc 9 · cool 7`
- **The Triage Officer** _(French trier, 'to sort'; medical/military triage = sorting casualties by what's worth treating now.)_ — Triage is universally read as 'decide what gets attention and what doesn't,' a near-perfect gloss of the worth-building filter. Loses a notch only because it's lighter on the sharpening-the-spec half.  `desc 9 · cool 6`
- **The Brief Forger** _('Brief' = a sharpened statement of the job to be done (advertising/legal/design); 'forge' = to hammer raw material into a finished tool.)_ — Directly names the 'fuzzy wish → sharp job' transformation: it forges a brief out of a vague ask. High descriptiveness with a craft-forge ring that keeps it cool — and a nice nod to the dropped 'factory' framing without saying it.  `desc 8 · cool 8`
- **The Greenlighter** _(Film-industry term — the executive who 'greenlights' a project, authorizing it to go into production.)_ — Instantly reads as 'the one who decides yes/no on whether a thing gets made' — maximally legible for the decider half. Drops a little because it says nothing about sharpening the spec.  `desc 8 · cool 7`
- **The Mandate Smith** _(Latin mandatum, 'a commission/charge to act'; 'smith' from the metal-shaping craft tradition.)_ — It forges a mandate — turning a wish into an authorized, sharply-defined charge to build. Captures both the authorize-it and the shape-it sides, with a strong craft-guild sound.  `desc 8 · cool 8`
- **The Feasibility Oracle** _('Feasibility study' (engineering/business gate) + 'oracle' (Greek/myth source of binding judgment).)_ — States the literal worth-it function — judge whether it's feasible and worth building — with a mythic verdict-giver flavor that adds variety from a different source than the rest.  `desc 8 · cool 7`

### THE SIGHT / SEER
_reads and maps the ENTIRE project into a knowledge graph before anyone acts — it SEES the whole codebase (this is graphify)_

- **The Cartographer** _(Latin/Greek 'charta' (map) + 'graphos' (writer) — one who draws maps of unknown territory.)_ — Top pick: a cartographer surveys terrain and renders it as a navigable map — which is literally what graphify does (maps the whole codebase into a navigable graph). High descriptiveness AND high cool, and the map metaphor maps perfectly onto a knowledge graph. A smart person instantly guesses 'this thing maps the territory.'  `desc 9 · cool 8`
- **The Topographer** _(Greek 'topos' (place) + 'graphein' (to write) — one who maps the detailed features and relief of a place and how they connect.)_ — Even more precise than cartographer for a GRAPH: topography is about mapping features AND their relief/connections — i.e., nodes and how they relate. 'Maps the structure and relationships of the whole terrain' is exactly a knowledge graph. Slightly more obscure word, hence ranked just under Cartographer.  `desc 9 · cool 7`
- **The Surveyor** _(Anglo-French 'surveier' (to look over) — the licensed professional who measures land and fixes its boundaries before any building begins.)_ — Nails the 'before anyone acts' clause: a surveyor maps the entire site BEFORE construction. Maximally on-function for a pre-action mapping role; loses a point to Cartographer/Topographer only because surveying is about measurement/boundaries more than relationship-graphs.  `desc 9 · cool 6`
- **The Indexer** _(Latin 'index' (pointer/list) — one who reads a whole corpus and builds the cross-referenced map of where each thing lives and how it links.)_ — Strikingly literal for graphify: an indexer reads EVERYTHING and builds the cross-referenced catalog of where each thing is and how it connects — that IS the knowledge graph. Software-native (database indexes, code indexers), so the audience guesses the function immediately. Lower cool, high precision.  `desc 9 · cool 5`
- **The Pathfinder** _(American frontier term — one who discovers and maps routes through unknown territory for others to follow.)_ — Captures the EDGES of the graph: a pathfinder traces routes and connections through unmapped ground so others can follow — exactly relationships/paths in a knowledge graph. Strong cool + clearly descriptive of mapping connections, and it explicitly serves the actors who come after.  `desc 8 · cool 8`
- **The Scout** _(Old French 'escouter' (to listen/observe) — the advance agent who explores and maps unknown ground ahead of the party.)_ — Best of the recon family: a scout goes in FIRST, observes the whole layout, and reports it so others can act with knowledge — the pre-action explorer in one crisp word. Beats Recon/Lookout/Overwatch on legibility and beats Oracle/Eye because it actually maps rather than just 'sees.'  `desc 8 · cool 7`
- **The Reconnaissance / Recon** _(Military French 'reconnaissance' (recognition) — scouting that maps the field before the main force moves.)_ — Whole job is to see and report the entire terrain before any action — a tight fit for 'map everything before anyone acts.' Kept for source variety (military) and high cool; ranked last of the seven because it overlaps Scout and is a hair less specific about producing a persistent map.  `desc 8 · cool 8`

### THE PLANNER / ARCHITECT
_breaks the intent into the recursive tree and freezes the contracts — the one who designs the plan_

- **The Architect** _(Latin/Greek 'arkhitekton' — chief (arkhi) builder/craftsman (tekton). The master who draws the blueprint others build from.)_ — The single most legible match: an architect designs the blueprint before anyone builds, which IS 'designs the plan.' Universally understood across every audience, high enough cool, and the role's own name uses it. The benchmark every other candidate is measured against.  `desc 10 · cool 6`
- **The Recursion Architect** _(Hybrid: 'recursion' (CS — a structure defined in terms of itself, the self-similar tree) + Architect.)_ — Spells out the EXACT mechanism: it designs the recursive plan tree. The most literal match to 'breaks the intent into the recursive tree.' Loses to plain Architect only because the compound reads engineered and is narrower in audience, but it telegraphs the recursion that defines this role.  `desc 10 · cool 6`
- **The Specwright** _(Coined: 'spec' (specification — the frozen contract) + '-wright' (maker, as in shipwright). The maker of specifications.)_ — Tightly names both halves: it WRIGHTS (crafts) the SPEC (the binding, frozen contract). Engineers read 'spec' as the authoritative plan instantly, and the craft-suffix telegraphs 'maker.' Captures the freeze-the-contracts duty better than Architect; docked only for niche legibility outside engineering.  `desc 9 · cool 7`
- **The Engineer of Record** _(Construction/civil term — the EOR is the licensed engineer who owns, signs, and freezes the official design all parties are contractually bound to.)_ — 'Of Record' precisely telegraphs the freeze-the-contracts, authoritative-and-binding duty that the other design names miss. Weighty and unambiguous. Slightly corporate and long, and 'engineer' is broad, so it sits just below the spec/recursion specialists.  `desc 9 · cool 6`
- **The Contractwright** _(Coined: 'contract' (the binding interface) + '-wright' (Old English 'wryhta,' a maker — shipwright, playwright). One who fashions the contracts.)_ — Names both halves like Specwright but foregrounds the CONTRACTS that get frozen — the second clause of the role. The craft-suffix telegraphs 'maker of contracts.' Slightly less idiomatic than 'spec' to engineers, and overlaps Specwright, so ranked just under it.  `desc 9 · cool 8`
- **The Decomposer** _(Latin 'de-' (apart) + 'componere' (to put together) — to break a whole into parts; the CS term for splitting a problem into sub-problems.)_ — Literally names the core act: breaking intent into a tree of sub-parts ('recursive decomposition'). Maximally on-function for the first clause and instantly read by technical people. Drops below the design/contract names only because it ignores the freeze-the-contracts half and reads a bit clinical.  `desc 10 · cool 5`
- **The Schematist** _(Greek 'skhema' (form, figure, plan) — one who produces the schema/schematic, the formal structural diagram of a system.)_ — 'Schema' directly means the structural plan, so a schematist designs the structure — telegraphs the design-the-plan function well and adds source variety (Greek/scientific). Kept over Blueprinter/Drafter because 'schema' also evokes the formal structural tree; docked for being a slightly obscure word.  `desc 8 · cool 6`

### THE CONTRACT / BLUEPRINT
_the frozen spec (types, schema, failing tests) every worker builds against — the binding promise that makes the pieces fit_

- **The Contract** _(Latin 'contractus' — drawn together; in software a literal term of art (interface contracts, design-by-contract, API contracts).)_ — Best fit for this exact role: the binding promise the pieces must honor. Engineers hear it and instantly map it to the agreed interface, and laypeople hear 'binding agreement everyone is held to.' It also carries the project's own chosen framing ('the binding promise that makes the pieces fit'). Maximally descriptive with real spark.  `desc 9 · cool 6`
- **The Schematic** _(Greek 'schema' — form/figure; the electronics term for the authoritative wiring diagram.)_ — Says 'the exact diagram every part must connect to' with engineering precision blueprint lacks. Telegraphs 'build/wire to this exactly' and feels more technical and cooler than its plainer cousins. Strong descriptiveness + above-average cool.  `desc 8 · cool 6`
- **The Blueprint** _(19th-c. cyanotype reproduction process for architectural drawings (white lines on blue).)_ — Universally understood as 'the master plan every builder works from.' Zero explanation needed and on-brand for The Tower's building metaphor. Slightly generic, which is why it trails the sharper Contract/Schematic.  `desc 9 · cool 5`
- **The Specification** _(Latin 'specificare' — to make specific; the engineering/legal term for a binding statement of required properties.)_ — The plainest possible read — it literally IS the spec everyone builds to, including types/schema/tests. Perfect descriptiveness, but nearly zero spark keeps it mid-pack against equally-clear, cooler options.  `desc 10 · cool 3`
- **The Master Pattern** _(Tailoring/casting/manufacturing — the authoritative template every copy is cut or cast from.)_ — 'The single authoritative template all pieces are made to match' is exactly the role, and the craft/guild origin adds source variety the list needs. 'Pattern' is a touch softer than 'types/tests,' but the function still telegraphs cleanly.  `desc 8 · cool 5`
- **The Interface** _(Latin 'inter' + 'facies' — between-faces; in CS, the declared boundary/contract a component must implement.)_ — For engineers this IS the word for 'the agreed surface pieces fit against' — maximally descriptive in-domain. Keeps a programmer-native source in the set; docked only because it's jargon-flat to outsiders and overlaps Contract.  `desc 9 · cool 4`
- **The Keystone** _(Architecture — the wedge-shaped stone at an arch's apex that locks all other stones in place.)_ — Telegraphs 'the one piece that makes every other piece fit and hold' — a strong functional metaphor for a binding spec, and the coolest title that still describes the role. Implies 'last' rather than 'frozen-first,' which is the only knock keeping it at #7.  `desc 6 · cool 8`

### THE BUILDERS / WORKER SWARM
_the many autonomous agents that build the leaves in parallel and LEVEL UP over runs_

- **Journeymen** _(Medieval guild system — the rank between apprentice and master; a qualified worker who does the actual building and ADVANCES toward master with experience.)_ — Uniquely nails BOTH halves of the role in one word: 'they do the actual building' AND 'they level up over runs' (apprentice→journeyman→master is a built-in competence ladder). No other candidate encodes the leveling trait while staying a true builder term. A smart listener instantly reads 'working builders who climb in skill.'  `desc 9 · cool 6`
- **Fabricators** _(Latin 'fabricare'; modern manufacturing/engineering term for the line workers/machines that fabricate parts to spec, and a Warhammer 40k Adeptus Mechanicus title.)_ — A fab shop IS parallel build cells producing finished parts to spec — the listener maps it directly onto 'the units that build the pieces.' Maximally legible, industrially exact, and carries autonomous-machine connotations that fit agents.  `desc 9 · cool 6`
- **Forgers** _(Old French 'forgier', Latin 'fabricare'; a smith who hammers raw material into finished form, repeatedly reheating and reworking.)_ — Forging literally means 'make the thing,' and the heat-hammer-temper-repeat loop telegraphs the level-up-each-pass trait better than any other smith term. High descriptiveness plus the most heat of the front-runners. (Minor: 'forger' also means counterfeiter, but context kills that read.)  `desc 8 · cool 8`
- **Constructor Swarm** _(Latin 'construere' (to build together) + 'swarm' from biology/robotics swarm intelligence (many simple agents, parallel, emergent).)_ — The two load-bearing facts — 'constructor' (builds) and 'swarm' (many-in-parallel) — are both literally in the name with zero guessing. The most unambiguous descriptor of the parallel-build trait; loses points only on flatness.  `desc 10 · cool 5`
- **Artificers** _(Latin 'artifex' (maker/craftsman); a D&D class and a real word for a skilled maker — in militaries, the soldier-tradesman who builds and repairs munitions.)_ — Plainly 'the ones who make things'; an artificer is by definition a hands-on builder, so a smart listener guesses 'these construct the actual artifacts.' Strong cool-to-descriptive balance and a real, recognizable craft word.  `desc 8 · cool 7`
- **Assembler Drones** _(Manufacturing 'assembler' (puts parts into a whole) + 'drone' (worker bee / autonomous unit; Borg drones build collectively).)_ — Both halves spell out the role: 'assembler' = builds the finished thing from parts, 'drone' = autonomous worker unit operating in a hive (parallel). Clearly telegraphs swarm-of-builders; slightly behind on the level-up read.  `desc 9 · cool 6`
- **Worker Castes** _(Eusocial-insect biology — ant/bee/termite colonies have a 'worker caste' that does all the building and foraging in massive parallelism.)_ — 'Worker' nails the function and 'caste/colony' nails the swarm-of-many; everyone knows worker ants build the nest in parallel, so the parallel-builder read is instant and intuitive.  `desc 9 · cool 5`

### THE INSPECTOR / BREAKER
_attacks finished work to try to break it; a piece is DONE only when it survives the assault_

- **The Stress-Tester** _(Materials engineering — stress testing loads a component to and past its limits to find where it fractures before it ships.)_ — Maximum clarity: a smart listener instantly knows it applies force to finished work to find the breaking point. The role IS the name — impossible to mishear. Lower cool ceiling, but the rule says clarity wins, and nothing here describes the function more exactly.  `desc 10 · cool 4`
- **The Breaker** _(Plain English / hacker culture ('breaking' software) — and the role name in the prompt itself.)_ — One unambiguous word: its job is to break the thing, and only what it can't break is done. Reads cool and stays perfectly literal — the rare title that is both. Names the exact done-criterion.  `desc 9 · cool 7`
- **The Red Team** _(Military/cybersecurity — the 'red team' role-plays the enemy and attacks your own defenses to expose weaknesses before a real adversary does.)_ — Term of art understood broadly to mean 'whose job is to attack us and find the holes.' Survive-the-attack is baked into the concept, and it carries real-world authority. High clarity, high cool, widely legible.  `desc 8 · cool 8`
- **The Adversary** _(Cryptography/security — the 'adversary model' is the formal attacker a system must withstand; a design is secure only if it survives the adversary's best assault.)_ — Hearing 'Adversary' you immediately know its job is to attack the work and that surviving it is the bar. A precise standard term that still sounds menacing and cool.  `desc 8 · cool 8`
- **The Pen-Tester** _(Cybersecurity — 'penetration testing' authorizes an expert to attack a system the way an intruder would, to prove it can or can't be breached.)_ — Industry-standard and self-explanatory: paid to attack the finished thing and report what breaks; passing the test is the certification. Slightly jargon, but the function is dead obvious.  `desc 8 · cool 7`
- **The Falsifier** _(Philosophy of science (Popper) — a theory is sound only if it survives genuine attempts at falsification; you try hardest to prove it WRONG.)_ — Names the exact method: actively try to disprove the work, and only the unfalsified passes. Reads a touch academic, but it describes the mechanism precisely and brings a source the rest of the list lacks.  `desc 8 · cool 6`
- **The Proof Gate** _(Mathematics/logic — a 'proof' is a claim that has survived rigorous attempted refutation; a 'gate' is a pass/fail checkpoint nothing crosses unless it qualifies.)_ — States the function directly: nothing is DONE until it passes through and is proven against attack. The gate framing telegraphs the survive-or-fail verdict and the done-criterion explicitly.  `desc 8 · cool 7`

### THE ROUTER / DISPATCHER
_assigns each task to the right model (Claude vs Codex), balances load, fails over when one runs out_
