1 · The Two Maxims

Doctrine, not software — files, not models.

Everything in the force obeys two laws:

"Build nothing the model curve will obsolete.
Build only what compounds as models improve."

"The compounding lives in the files, not the model." Every proven self-improvement mechanism makes a frozen, cheaper model perform like a stronger one did. That's the exit strategy for the day the strongest model gets expensive — measured, not hoped.

⚙️ We build none of

Orchestrator daemons · model routers · logging pipelines · worker swarm managers · custom state stores. Every model generation makes them less necessary.

📚 The system IS

Orders · sieged rubrics · the Archive · doctrine deltas · minted skills · Service Records — files that every smarter model executes better, and every cheaper model is lifted by.

2 · How a Mission Runs

One campaign. Five moves.

From "I want X" to a shipped, battle-tested X — with exactly one spend gate for you.

01
📋

Move OneThe Briefing

The General interrogates you — one question at a time — scouts the terrain, queries the Archive for precedent and the Armory for proven assets, then writes the Orders: one page — mission, victory conditions, how it'll be judged, guardrails, cost estimate, cut-lines. "If your idea is vague, your output is vague. You are the founder." The heaviest move, on purpose.

answer questions · sharpen what you actually want
02
🚦

Move TwoGo / No-Go

The one spend gate. You edit the Orders file directly and say go. Nothing spends before that word. Mid-build changes arrive as FRAGOs — formal amendments, never silent drift. Every other human touchpoint is declared in the Orders up front.

edit one file · say "go"
03
🔨

Move ThreeThe Build

One strong agent builds end-to-end by default. A working skeleton link lands in the first hour (First Contact), then 📨 Dispatches — reports from the front at every checkpoint, async, never blocking. Escalation to squads happens on measured tripwires, never feelings (an agent can't feel its own decline; instruments don't lie). Always shippable at every cut-line.

nothing — read Dispatches, steer if you feel like it
04
💥

Move FourThe Siege

A rival AI from a different model family attacks under a formal Siege packet — kill authority, praise forbidden, severity-ranked findings. Every finding gets a recorded verdict: fixed, rejected, or accepted-risk. Done = survived. A builder grading its own homework is most confident exactly when it's most wrong.

nothing — the attacker works for you
05
🎓

Move FiveThe Debrief

The campaign cannot close without its After-Action Report — written from the attacker's kill-log, not the builder's self-story. Metrics go to the Service Record; lessons land as small typed deltas (never rewrites); anything reusable that survived gets minted into the Armory. The next campaign starts measurably smarter.

collect your finished artifact 🎁
🗡️

Skirmish

Small, safe, reversible → the moves collapse. Most days are skirmishes — no ceremony for small jobs.

⚔️

Campaign

The full five moves, for anything sizable, novel, or dangerous.

🔍

Probe loop

Don't know what you want yet? Cheap probes iterate until the Orders are knowable — then the moves run.

And missions that never end — a product, a store, a pipeline — run as 🏰 Standing Operations: a "Good morning, Commander" brief every day, a weekly review, pivot councils when something underperforms, campaigns spawned for every bounded chunk. Campaigns build; operations run.

4 · Force Levels & The Fallback

Right-sized force — and no model it can't survive.

Seats are roles; models are config. The General's chair holds whatever brain is strongest and affordable — and the structure compensates when the brain weakens.

F1Solo Strike

The General builds it alone, end-to-end Default

Maximum coherence, zero coordination tax. The F1 bar floats with the seat's strength.

F2Strike Team

+ parallel specialists for side-work

Research sweeps, competing variants, councils. Parallel work freezes its boundary contract first.

F3Full Deployment

True decomposition — squads in isolated worktrees

For huge targets, hard deadlines — and for weaker seats. A weaker General takes smaller solo scopes, decomposes earlier, freezes more contracts, sieges harder: quality held by structure when it can't be held by brains. The swarm was never deleted — it's the fallback gear.

Fallback chains are named: General Fable → Opus → Sonnet · Siege Codex → any rival family (alien-ness is the requirement, not the brand). Before the Fable window closes, the same benchmark campaign runs on both seats — the fallback posture is measured, not guessed.

5 · The Trust Engine

Done = survived. Not "tests pass."

🎖️ The Builder

Builds to the Orders; runs every mechanical gate — types, tests, build, e2e. Table stakes, not the verdict.

💥 The Demolition Squad

A different model family, formally granted kill authority. Hunts what the builder can't see: missing cases, wrong assumptions, tests that lie. Every finding dispositioned in a ledger — nothing waved off silently.

Survives the assault → ships. Doesn't → back to the line.

For work with no tests — research, decisions, taste — the judging rubric is frozen before building and attacked before go; anything the world answers later goes in a verdicts ledger with a check-back date. The force never grades its own taste.

6 · The Intelligence Division

Self-improvement with machinery — and a scoreboard.

Not a promise. Four organs, each stolen from a system with published evidence or a profitable field deployment:

📚 The Archive

Everything is recorded — every order, log, attack, and especially every piece of your feedback with its reason. Indexed so the right precedent surfaces at every Briefing — and precedent that keeps working rises automatically; landfill decays out.

🧠 The Analyst

A standing agent that runs nightly, in the cloud: consolidates lessons, grades old predictions against reality, watches the metrics for drift, and proposes doctrine improvements as PRs you approve. The force has a member whose whole job is making the force better.

🎯 Boot Camp

New kinds of work start supervised: approve / reject / iterate, reasons required, all remembered — until the rejection rate falls below a written threshold and the class graduates. Proven in the field: a few hundred verdicts over days, then trust. You watch the curve fall.

🏭 The Armory's Forge

Campaigns that survive can mint weapons: templates, rubrics, whole skills — admitted only on survival, ranked by usefulness, composable. The Military is a skill that creates smaller skills. Third-party capability? Replicated in-house, never installed.

📊 The Service Record settles it honestly: kill-rate, estimate accuracy, intervention rate, cost — plotted per campaign. If the curves don't fall, it isn't improving — and the doctrine contains its own decommission criterion. No sunk-cost shrines.

9 · Rules of Engagement

Autonomous, never reckless.

Danger — not size — sets how carefully it moves, and it dials everything: siege depth, report cadence, formality gear, loop thresholds.

🟢 Green

Reversible & isolated. Direct writes, skirmish gear, full speed.

🟡 Amber

Touches something live. Everything lands as a proposal — a PR you merge. It proposes; you decide.

🔴 Red

Payments, auth, migrations, deletions. Proposal mode + your confirmation per action + protected paths it may never touch.

Always armed: cost caps, clock caps, a hypothesis-watching loop detector, a real campaign lock, a one-page audit of every key every agent holds — and a monthly ceiling for the whole force with a defined degradation order. Autonomy without an automatic abort is a runaway hazard, so there is always an abort.
