The standing law throughout: the doctrine freeze holds until two campaigns have produced War Logs, AARs, and Service Record rows. Findings from this tribunal are not folded into doctrine now — that's the ratchet. They wait, on this page, for the data to agree or overrule them. Campaign 3 (a Rung-2 decision memo — the unexercised half of the Verdict Ladder) queues behind the benchmark.

Why this isn't the ratchet in convoy formation: the unanimous prescription of all four judges was contact — Opus: "run one real campaign this week"; Codex: "three boring campaigns." The ratchet is doctrine production; the freeze stops exactly that. And the statistical-mass charge is engaged, not dodged: the first decisions don't need 30-campaign curves — they need existence proofs (did the cross-family Siege catch anything the gates missed? what did the ceremony cost?), which two campaigns supply as binary facts. If time runs short before 06-22: Campaign 1 + the benchmark are the core; Campaign 2 slips to the Opus seat — which is itself the exit-thesis data.

The kill switch (set before the pilot, so it can't be argued with after): the doctrine FAILS Campaign 1 if (a) the Siege kills nothing the mechanical gates missed, (b) the ceremony's overhead exceeds what it caught (Orders + AAR cost vs. a bare build), or (c) the AAR changes nothing about how Campaign 2 would run. Any two of three → Road 4 goes live: the costume stands down, the four verbs stay as silent habits, and The Tower gets every remaining hour. Negative data wins; that is the whole point.
