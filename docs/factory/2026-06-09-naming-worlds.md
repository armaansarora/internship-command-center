## 🧪 Hybrids (frankenstein the best parts)

- **SPIRAL ARISE** — SPIRAL's name + Solo Leveling's worker (the Shadow) + Gurren Lagann's nesting unit (the Drill). The system is SPIRAL; defeated sub-problems rise as Shadow workers; each Shadow wields a Drill that bores into a sub-problem and spawns a smaller Drill. _Marries the two perfect-10s. 'Spiral' is a clean ownable one-word brand AND a literal description of the drill-within-a-drill geometry; 'Arise' gives you the verb for raising a finished piece plus the across-run leveling. Name the unit 'the Drill' and the swarm 'Shadows' and every trait has a noun._
- **THE HATCHERY (Wish-cast workers)** — StarCraft's Hatchery/Larva onion + D&D Wish's declare-it-true North Star. The North Star command is a Wish ('let this be TRUE'); the build unit is the Larva that morphs into a Hatchery to spawn child Larvae for sub-problems. _Hatchery gives you the cleanest recursion noun in the whole list (Larva to Hatchery to Larva), and grafting Wish's declarative intent fixes Zerg's one weakness — Zerg swarms, it doesn't declare. Now you have both: speak the strain as fact, the onion makes it flesh._
- **GAIA / CAULDRON** — Horizon's GAIA master-mind (North Star + named subfunctions) + Horizon's Cauldron as the explicit recursive unit + Westworld's Cradle (INSPECTION: run it in sim until fidelity). GAIA is the cockpit intelligence; the Cauldron is the build-cell that fabricates Cauldrons; nothing ships until its Cradle sim can't break it. _GAIA nails North Star + named-swarm + recursion, but 'subfunction' is a weak unit word. 'Cauldron' is a far cooler unit noun and is canonically the thing that builds machines that build machines. Bolt on the Cradle and you've covered INSPECTION too — three traits, three crisp nouns._
- **CRUCIBLE PROTOCOL** — Crucible's ownable one-word brand (the banned-word cousin of 'foundry') + Cyberpunk's Daemon as the worker/breach term + Conway's Constructor as the self-replicating unit. You pour intent into the Crucible; Daemons breach sub-systems; each carries a Constructor that builds a copy of itself plus its blueprint. _Crucible is a genuinely ownable neon-forge brand with zero anime baggage — board-room safe. 'Daemon' is the perfect agent word (UNIX + Cyberpunk both back it), and the Von Neumann Constructor gives you literal self-replication as the recursive unit. Clean, technical, sounds like real infrastructure._
- **PRIME RADIANT** — Foundation's Prime Radiant (SIGHT — the living crystal of equations you zoom into infinitely) + Foundation's Vault (North Star — predict the finished thing, make the math come true) + Steins;Gate's Convergence (the swarm converges on the declared attractor across iterations). _This is the COCKPIT-and-SIGHT hybrid. The Prime Radiant is the single pane of glass where every macro-prediction unfolds into nested sub-equations — that IS your knowledge graph you can see. Convergence supplies the 'every run remembers the last' leveling. Reads as a serious, almost scientific product name._
- **ATHANOR** — Hermetic alchemy's Athanor (the self-feeding furnace that runs the four-stage Opus and decants a smaller Athanor inside itself) + FMA's Transmutation Circle as the frozen Contract + the maxim solve et coagula as the decompose/recombine verb. _If you want a name nobody else will ever pick, Athanor is it — a self-feeding furnace whose whole identity is 'a complete furnace inside the furnace.' The Transmutation Circle gives you the frozen-blueprint Contract; solve et coagula gives you a two-word slogan for the recursion (dissolve the sub-problem, recombine up the chain). Esoteric and gorgeous._

## 🃏 Wildcards

- ROOT / FORKBOMB (UNIX hacker lore) — the least metaphorical pick on the board: fork() until the whole process tree says TRUE. The PID tree literally IS the build, every node identical to its parent owning a smaller slice. Engineers GET it instantly, no lore tax. 'forge' is banned but 'fork' is right there.
- THE WIRED / LAIN (Serial Experiments Lain) — 'you're all just me.' When a problem's too big she instantiates another Lain to handle a sub-layer, and that one is fully herself, just deeper in. A self inside a self inside a self where every layer can act as the whole. Eerie, cult, unforgettable.
- MYCELIA (fungal networks) — whisper the fruit you want, the network grows it underground. The Hypha forks into identical hyphae, the colony is one fractal thread, and it fruits a Sporocarp (finished artifact) wherever conditions are met. Organic, non-tech, and the 'Wood Wide Web' framing sells the autonomous swarm without a single robot.
- THE LIFE ENGINE (Conway's Game of Life) — set the seed, declare the still-life, watch it build itself. The Von Neumann universal constructor reads a blueprint and builds a copy of itself INCLUDING the blueprint — the Gosper gun firing guns that fire guns. The most rigorous, provable recursion in the entire list; mathematicians will swoon.
- THE CATHEDRAL OF SHADOWS (SMT/Persona) — fuse two demons, get a stronger one, repeat until reality obeys. A Special Fusion is secretly a fusion-tree of fusion-trees down to the first demon in the Compendium. The 'combine outputs into a stronger unit + remember everything in the Compendium' model maps shockingly well onto self-improving builds.
- THE ALEPH (Borges) — one point that contains every point. Drill into any Aleph-Point and you find a complete Aleph inside it; the whole system is nested in each of its parts. The Library of Babel does it at scale. The single most intellectually flattering name you could ship — infinite-in-a-point as the entire pitch.
